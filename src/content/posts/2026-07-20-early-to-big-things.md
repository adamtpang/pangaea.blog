I was on ChatGPT day one. Network School, day one. A handful of other things that turned out to matter, day one.

People call that luck. Sometimes it is. But it happens too often to be only luck, and I think the pattern is teachable, so let me try.

Being early is not about predicting the future. It is about lowering your own activation energy to try the new thing before it is comfortable. Most people wait for consensus, for the review, for the friend who already uses it. By then the edge is gone. The edge was the discomfort.

So the method is small: when something new appears and a quiet part of you says huh, go touch it that day. Not to invest, not to commit. Just to feel it with your own hands while the crowd is still deciding whether it is allowed to be excited.

Most of what you touch will be nothing. That is the cost, and it is cheap. You only need to be right a few times, and you cannot be right early if you keep arriving late.

Curiosity is the whole engine. If you are interested, you will be early. If you are early enough, often enough, people start to say you see the future. You do not. You just showed up on day one.
