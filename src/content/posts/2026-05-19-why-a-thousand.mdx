In the last post I said the plan is 1,000. People keep asking if that is realistic. Wrong question.

The number is not a target. It is a forcing function. A thousand is large enough that talent cannot carry it and small enough that consistency can. You do not write a thousand good essays. You write a thousand essays, and somewhere in the doing, the good ones start happening to you.

Listen to this while you read the rest.

<Spotify uri="track/5626KdflSKfeDK7RJQfSrE" />

Nils Frahm takes one small phrase and repeats it for eight minutes. Nothing about bar four tells you it will become a cathedral by bar two hundred. The accumulation is the composition. The repetition is not in service of the thing; the repetition is the thing.

That is the whole bet of Pangaea. Not that any single post matters, but that a thousand of them, stacked, become a shape I could not have designed up front. A worldview you can only get to by walking, not by planning.

<Quote attribution="Will Durant, summarizing Aristotle">
We are what we repeatedly do. Excellence, then, is not an act but a habit.
</Quote>

So: weekly at the floor, daily when the current is good. Not because 1,000 is sacred, but because showing up is.

Onward.
