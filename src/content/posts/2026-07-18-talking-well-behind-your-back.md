There is a kind of gossip nobody warns you about: the good kind.

Most of what we mean by talking behind someone's back is a knife. But the same privacy that hides a knife can hide a gift. You can praise a person in a room they will never enter, to people who will never tell them. No audience, no credit, no angle. Just the truth about someone, said warmly, when it can buy you nothing.

I love talking well behind people's backs.

It is the cleanest test of what you actually think. Praise to someone's face can be currency: you might be buying goodwill, softening a favor, angling for the compliment back. Praise behind their back is spent on nothing. It cannot reach them, so it cannot be a transaction. It is just belief, leaking out when no one is keeping score.

And it does reach them, eventually, the way weather reaches everything. People can feel the temperature of the rooms they are not in. Someone always carries it back.

So say the good thing when they are not there. If you would not say I love you to your friend, are they even your friend?

Talk well behind my back. I will talk well behind yours.
