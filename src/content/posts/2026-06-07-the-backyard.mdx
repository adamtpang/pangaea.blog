My parents divorced when I was ten.

For ten years after, family was a logistics problem. Two houses. Two calendars. A kid with a backpack, moving between them.

Then one afternoon, in the backyard, under a bright blue sky, the three of us were standing together. And they hugged me. Both of them. At the same time.

That is the whole memory. A hug in a backyard.

But for as long as it lasted, everything that had come apart was, briefly, back together. Not fixed. Not undone. Whole. Ten years of two houses, and for a moment there was one place, and I was standing in the middle of it, and nothing in the world was missing.

A hug ends. You let go. Everyone goes back inside. But I got to stand in the center of my whole family once and feel the world be complete. That happened. It is still true that it happened.
