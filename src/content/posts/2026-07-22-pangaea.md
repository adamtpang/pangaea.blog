Two hundred million years ago there was one continent. Every piece of land on Earth, touching. Then it broke, and the pieces drifted, and now there are islands.

I was born on one of them.

Guam is thirty miles long. You can drive its whole length in an afternoon. From the air it is a green comma in an enormous blue nothing, closer to Tokyo than to any part of the country that owns it. Growing up there teaches you a specific thing, which is that the world is real but it is happening somewhere else. Everything arrives late. Movies, products, news, weather that already hit the Philippines. You learn the shape of being downstream.

I think that is the first fact about me. Not that I am from a small place, but that I grew up watching a bigger world arrive on a delay and quietly deciding I would rather be where it started.

## The break

My parents divorced when I was ten.

For ten years after, family was a logistics problem. Two houses. Two calendars. A kid with a backpack, moving between them.

I have written about this once already and I do not want to write it twice, so I will say only the structural part here. When you are ten, your family is not a relationship. It is a place. It is the ground. And when it comes apart, what you actually lose is not the people. You keep the people. You lose the ground.

What you get instead is a skill, which is the ability to be fine in two places and fully at home in neither. That skill is genuinely useful. I have used it constantly. It is also the reason I have spent most of my adult life building rooms for other people to stand in.

## Making things that hold people

I finished high school in 2020, which was a strange year to finish anything, and started writing music seriously.

I want to be precise about why. It was not a career plan. It was that a song is the smallest possible thing that can hold more than one person at a time. You make it alone in a room and then it exists somewhere between you and whoever hears it. For someone who had spent a decade shuttling between two houses, a place that could contain several people at once and did not require anybody to agree on anything was a very appealing kind of object.

Then in 2022 I graduated App Academy and became a software engineer, which is the same instinct with better tooling. An app is a room. You build it alone and then strangers stand in it.

I do not think I understood that these were the same activity until much later. At the time it just looked like I could not decide what I was.

## Arriving on day one

I was on ChatGPT day one. Network School, day one. A handful of other things that turned out to matter, day one.

People call that luck. I have argued elsewhere that it is a practiced skill, and I believe that. But the honest version, the one that belongs in a life story rather than an essay about method, is that being early is what being downstream turns into if you get angry about it early enough.

I grew up on a delay. So now when something new appears and a quiet part of me says huh, I go touch it that day. Not because I am good at predicting the future. Because I spent my childhood on the receiving end of it and I would rather not do that again.

## The island I picked

In 2025 I moved to Network School full-time and became longtermer number two.

The joke I did not notice for almost a year is that I left an island to live on an island. Langkawi is also thirty-something miles across. It is also a green shape in a lot of blue. From the air, honestly, it looks like home.

The difference is not the geography. It is that this one is full on purpose. Five hundred founders came here to be in the same place at the same time, which is the exact thing that does not happen naturally to people who grow up scattered. Guam was an island I was born on. This is an island I chose, and more importantly it is one that other people also chose, at the same time, deliberately.

That is the whole difference between being stranded and being gathered.

## What I have actually been building

I ship a lot of small things and for years I could not explain the pattern. Looking at them now, laid out, it is not subtle.

Wonderhall is a concert series. A room, on a schedule, where people are in the same place hearing the same thing.

Summon is a way to talk to the people who shaped you. Rockefeller, Deutsch, Sivers, Naval. Everyone I have learned from without ever being in a room with them.

Optimism is a map of what humanity wants, which is an attempt to put every person's demand on one surface so you can see it at once.

Pangaea is a blog named after the moment before the land broke.

I did not plan any of that. I just kept building the same object over and over with different materials, which is what people do when something is unfinished in them.

## The backyard

There is one more thing and then I will stop.

About ten years after the divorce, in a backyard, under a bright blue sky, my parents hugged me at the same time. Both of them. The three of us standing together.

That is the entire event. A hug in a backyard. It fixed nothing, undid nothing, and changed no arrangement. But for the length of it, everything that had come apart was back together, and I was standing in the middle of it.

Continents do not reassemble. Pangaea broke and it is not coming back, and the pieces are still drifting apart right now, about as fast as your fingernails grow. That is real and it is not sad. What happened instead is that things grew on the pieces.

So I am not trying to undo the break. I am aware that you cannot. I am doing the other thing, which is making land, and putting it where people can stand on it together, and staying interested in what grows.

I was born on an island. I live on one now. In between I learned that the only real cure for scattered is to build somewhere worth gathering.

That is the name. That is the whole story.
